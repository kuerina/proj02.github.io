Bio

Hello, My name is Hugo Trivino, and I am a computer engineer and science student from Chilean Patagonia. I joined the workforce at a young age after I graduated high school at age 17. I left Patagonia and traveled some two thousand miles north to the Atacama desert to work. For the next six years, I worked for different mining service companies including Sodexo, Compass-Group, and Esperanza, where I got my first taste of programming. Working on one of the largest copper mine projects in the world, I taught myself how to program so I could do the most efficient and reliable work possible. Now at IIT, I am learning how to formalize my self taught skills (my code was hard on the eyes), and discovering the intricacies of hardware design. I want to understand hardware and make abstract problems faster to compute by doing so. I am passionate about a lifelong journey of learning and build the skills needed to address the fascinating problems found in fields such as data mining, social network analysis, machine learning, artificial intelligence, and cybersecurity.
